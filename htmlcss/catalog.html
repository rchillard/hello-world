So, why is the above in a textarea tag? Well, I'll tell you: textarea is a raw text element, meaning it displays whatever appears inside of it as raw or plain text, even including HTML tags. The nice part is that you can display HTML tags inside of a textarea with very little extra work. This is important for demonstration and teaching persons. But how do you embed one textarea inside of another? Won't the browser think the first closing </textarea> tag is real and close the actual textarea? Yes, exactly. That's true! In order to get around this special case, you have escape the opening tag (left arrow) or < with a special sequence of characters.
